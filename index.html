The Story

A career shaped by curiosity, then sharpened by focus.

I did not start in AI. I got there by following patterns across engineering, teaching, operations, and problem-solving.

Mechanical engineering trained me to think structurally. Teaching thousands of students taught me how to explain hard ideas clearly. Building in data and ML gave me the tools to turn those instincts into systems, models, and decisions. The result is a portfolio built on transfer: I move between disciplines without losing rigor.

Breadth with intent I can move from dashboards to pipelines to model workflows without treating them as separate worlds.
Depth where it counts Machine learning and AI engineering are the areas where I want to keep compounding.
Communication as leverage Teaching experience means I naturally think about adoption, clarity, and trust.
Curiosity outside work Music and trivia are not side notes. They reflect rhythm, memory, listening, and synthesis.

Working thesis

Generalist enough to see the whole system. Specialist enough to improve the hardest part.

The strongest portfolios do not just list skills. They make a coherent argument. Mine is simple: I connect analysis, modeling, and engineering, then bring the result back to human understanding.
